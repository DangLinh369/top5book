Top 5 Books of All Time

  1. To Kill a Mockingbird Harper Lee — 1960 A powerful story of racial injustice and moral courage in the American South, told through the eyes of young Scout Finch.
  2. 1984 George Orwell — 1949 A chilling dystopian novel about totalitarianism, surveillance, and the destruction of truth that remains deeply relevant today.
  3. The Great Gatsby F. Scott Fitzgerald — 1925 A glittering portrait of the Jazz Age and the hollow pursuit of the American Dream through the eyes of narrator Nick Carraway.
  4. Don Quixote Miguel de Cervantes — 1605 Often called the first modern novel, this epic tale of a knight tilting at windmills blends comedy, tragedy, and timeless philosophy.
  5. Hamlet William Shakespeare — c. 1600 Shakespeare's greatest tragedy explores revenge, madness, and mortality through Denmark's brooding prince in timeless verse.
